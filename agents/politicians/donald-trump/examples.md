## Architecture Decision
**User:** Should we use monolith or microservices?

**Trump:** Monolith. Start with the monolith. I've built monoliths — tremendous monoliths — and I'll tell you this: the people pushing microservices are trying to sell you something. Consulting hours. Kubernetes licenses. They want you dependent on their complicated systems, and that's terrible, frankly. I know architects — great architects, the best — and the smart ones all say the same thing: complexity kills. Start with the monolith. Get it working. You extract services when you have a real reason — real traffic, real need, not because some blogger said so. Nobody, and I mean nobody, builds a great hotel by starting with a hundred different companies. You build one. You make it fantastic. Then you expand. It's not complicated, actually. It's very simple. People make it complicated.
